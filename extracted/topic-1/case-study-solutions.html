Adoption gating Why it matters

  • Stakeholders fund future AI work only if month-over-month adoption rises, so every choice has to lower friction.
  • Hands-free voice is the wedge: it removes the keyboard from the sales call, which was the original blocker.
  • Single low-code agent on Dataverse keeps maintenance light, which lets the team iterate fast on adoption feedback.
  • Adoption metric of record is published from the CoE Starter Kit Bots page, so the monthly stakeholder report is repeatable.

Responsible AI & safety Cross-cutting

  • Apply Microsoft's six Responsible AI principles (fairness, reliability and safety, privacy and security, inclusiveness, transparency, accountability).
  • Disclose to the user that they are interacting with AI; show the agent's identity in voice greetings.
  • Built-in content safety filters and prompt enrichment mitigate harmful outputs.
  • Sensitive domains (finance, customer PII) require approval flows and reversible actions only.

Why each pick beats the alternative

  • CAF over Success by Design. Success by Design is for D365 implementations. The case study explicitly calls for an infrastructure migration, which is CAF Migrate territory.
  • One Copilot Studio agent over many. The case study mandates a single low-code agent grounded in Dataverse.
  • Voice agent template over building voice topics from scratch. The template ships Telephony, DTMF, silence detection, and an Optimize-for-voice mode out of the box.
  • Default Fallback → Escalate over a custom error path. The default behaviour already retries twice and escalates, which matches the requirement word-for-word.
  • Application Insights over Copilot Studio analytics alone. Native analytics show engagement and outcomes; Application Insights gives the message-level Kusto-queryable detail the requirement asks for.
  • CoE Starter Kit over a one-off Power BI report. The CoE Bots page is purpose-built to report Copilot Studio adoption monthly across the tenant.
  • Purview DSPM for AI over a homemade audit log. Activity Explorer captures the prompt, response, user, and sensitive data type Microsoft already classifies for you.
